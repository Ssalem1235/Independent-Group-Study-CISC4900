PROJECT SUMMARY

  

    This project investigates the societal and environmental impacts of the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence using a data-driven and evidence-based approach. Drawing on publicly available datasets, it examines trends in AI adoption, the prevalence of AI exposure, and changes in measurable societal indicators. This project solely seeks to identify and quantify observable patterns that reflect how increasing AI integration intersects with social attitudes and behaviors. In addition, the project analyzes the environmental implications of AI growth by observing energy consumption and water usage trends caused by AI data centers. Through statistical analysis and data visualization, the project aims to provide clear insights into the broader societal and sustainability challenges posed by the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence.

MEET THE TEAM

  

    Saisha Wesley and Saleh Salem are senior undergraduate students in the Computer and Information Science department at Brooklyn College. This project was developed as part of their senior capstone, through which they chose to investigate a topic they are both deeply invested in, the broader societal and environmental consequences of artificial intelligence. Their interest in this subject stems from firsthand experience with AI's rapid expansion and prior academic engagement with its implications. Saisha's previous independent research on Anti-Blackness in Artificial Intelligence provided a foundational lens through which the harmful patterns embedded in AI systems were examined. This work served as a catalyst for exploring other dimensions of AI's impact on society and the environment. Driven by a shared commitment to transparency and public awareness, Saisha and Saleh designed this project to go beyond technical analysis. They created a publicly accessible, data-driven website aimed at shedding light on how the acceleration of AI is shaping our world in ways that demand critical attention. “We hope our work inspires and motivates our audience to make more conscious decisions regarding AI. Thank you.” — Message from the team.
